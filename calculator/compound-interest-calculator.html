Compound Interest Calculator

The Power of Compounding Explained

Compound interest is the addition of interest to the principal sum of a loan or deposit, or in other words, interest on interest. It is the result of reinvesting interest, rather than paying it out, so that interest in the next period is then earned on the principal sum plus previously accumulated interest.

Our Compound Interest Calculator India uses the standard formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where:

  • A is the total amount including interest.
  • P is the principal amount.
  • r is the annual interest rate (decimal).
  • n is the number of times interest is compounded per year.
  • t is the time the money is invested for.

Compounding can have a dramatic effect on the growth of an investment over time. Even small, regular contributions can grow into significant sums due to the power of compounding. Use this tool to plan your long-term financial goals, such as retirement or a child's education fund.
